📖 Chapter 1: Building with Bricks

Topics:

  • Understanding rows and columns in brick walls
  • Counting total bricks using multiplication
  • Identifying repeating patterns
  • Practical exercises: build small wall patterns

Examples:

  • 5 rows × 12 bricks = 60 bricks
  • Draw 4 rows × 8 bricks

Questions:

  • Q1: Count bricks in 7 rows × 9 bricks
  • Q2: Create a wall with 3 rows × 10 bricks
  • Q3: Identify patterns
  • Q4: Compare two walls for total bricks
  • Q5: Make your own brick pattern

📖 Chapter 2: Long and Short

Topics:

  • Measuring objects using ruler and tape
  • Comparing lengths: long vs short
  • Understanding cm and m
  • Estimating lengths before measuring
  • Practice exercises on daily objects

Examples:

  • Pencil 15 cm, Pen 12 cm → Pencil longer
  • Table 120 cm, Desk 100 cm → Table longer

Questions:

  • Q1: Measure your notebook
  • Q2: Compare 2 pencils
  • Q3: Which is longer: 1 m rope or 90 cm rope?
  • Q4: Draw object longer than pen
  • Q5: Estimate length of door

📖 Chapter 3: A Trip to Bhopal

Topics:

  • Distance and travel time
  • Reading simple maps
  • Calculating distance at constant speed
  • Estimating home to school distance
  • Practical map exercises

Examples:

  • Bus 20 km/hr × 3 hr → 60 km
  • Map scale 1cm=2km → 5cm on map = 10km

Questions:

  • Q1: Draw map home to park
  • Q2: Car travels 15 km/hr → 4 hr distance?
  • Q3: Compare two routes distance
  • Q4: Minutes for 30 km at 10 km/hr
  • Q5: Estimate distance in 30 min walk

📖 Chapter 4: Tick‑Tick‑Tick

Topics:

  • Reading analog clocks
  • Hours, minutes, seconds
  • Calculating time intervals
  • Drawing clocks practice
  • Daily life time calculation exercises

Examples:

  • 3:15 + 2 hr → 5:15
  • 1:30 + 45 min → 2:15

Questions:

  • Q1: Draw clock 7:45
  • Q2: Minutes in 3 hours
  • Q3: Add 30 min to 2:15
  • Q4: Duration 9:10 to 10:50
  • Q5: What time 2 hr 15 min after 5:30?

📖 Chapter 5: The Way The World Looks

Topics:

  • Recognizing 2D shapes (circle, triangle, square, rectangle)
  • Identifying shapes in the environment
  • Introduction to symmetry (line of symmetry)
  • Comparing shapes by sides and corners

Examples:

  • A triangle has 3 sides and 3 corners. A rectangle has 4 sides and 4 corners.
  • Draw a shape and find its line(s) of symmetry.

Questions:

  • Q1: List 5 objects at home that are circular.
  • Q2: Draw a rectangle and mark its corners.
  • Q3: Which of these are symmetrical: a square, a scalene triangle?
  • Q4: Draw a simple shape and draw its mirror line.
  • Q5: Count corners in a shape made of two squares joined.

📖 Chapter 6: The Junk Seller

Topics:

  • Understanding money: rupee and paise
  • Simple addition and subtraction with prices
  • Making change and comparing costs
  • Word problems about buying/selling

Examples:

  • Item A = ₹25, Item B = ₹40 → Total = ₹65
  • Pay ₹100 for ₹65 purchase → Change = ₹35

Questions:

  • Q1: If you buy 2 toys at ₹15 each, how much will you pay?
  • Q2: You have ₹50, you buy a book for ₹38. How much left?
  • Q3: Find the cost of 3 pencils at ₹7 each.
  • Q4: Which is cheaper: 2 apples at ₹12 or 3 apples at ₹18?
  • Q5: Make a small price list of 5 items and total their cost.

📖 Chapter 7: Jugs and Mugs

Topics:

  • Capacity: litres and millilitres
  • Comparing volumes (more or less)
  • Simple division of liquid into equal parts
  • Practical measuring with cups/jugs

Examples:

  • 2 L water into 4 mugs → 0.5 L (500 ml) per mug
  • 1000 ml = 1 L

Questions:

  • Q1: How many 250 ml cups make 1 L?
  • Q2: If a bottle has 1.5 L and you pour in 3 equal glasses, how much each?
  • Q3: Convert 750 ml into litres.
  • Q4: Which is greater: 800 ml or 0.8 L?
  • Q5: Fill a jug of 2 L into bottles of 500 ml — how many bottles?

📖 Chapter 8: Carts and Wheels

Topics:

  • Counting wheels of different vehicles
  • Multiplication as repeated addition
  • Grouping objects and quick counting
  • Simple word problems involving groups

Examples:

  • 5 carts × 4 wheels = 20 wheels
  • 7 cycles × 2 wheels = 14 wheels

Questions:

  • Q1: Count wheels for 6 cars (4 wheels each).
  • Q2: If 4 buses (6 wheels each), total wheels?
  • Q3: Use repeated addition to find 4 × 8.
  • Q4: Make a group problem using chairs and tables.
  • Q5: Draw 3 groups of 5 stars and count total.

📖 Chapter 9: Halves and Quarters

Topics:

  • Understanding 1/2 and 1/4 visually
  • Dividing shapes into equal parts
  • Simple fraction of objects (half of 8 apples)
  • Comparing halves and quarters

Examples:

  • Half of 8 = 4, Quarter of 8 = 2
  • 1 pizza divided into 4 → each = 1/4

Questions:

  • Q1: Divide 10 sweets into halves — how many pieces?
  • Q2: What is 1/4 of 12?
  • Q3: Shade half and quarter of a rectangle.
  • Q4: Which is bigger: 1/2 or 1/4?
  • Q5: If you cut a cake into 4 and eat 2 pieces, what fraction left?

📖 Chapter 10: Play with Patterns

Topics:

  • Recognizing repeating patterns
  • Extending patterns (shapes, colours, numbers)
  • Making your own patterns
  • Simple arithmetic patterns (odd/even)

Examples:

  • Red, Blue, Red, Blue → next = Red
  • 1,3,5,7 → next = 9 (odd number pattern)

Questions:

  • Q1: Continue the pattern: ★, ◆, ★, ◆, ?
  • Q2: Make a 6-step colour pattern using 2 colours.
  • Q3: Find the next three numbers: 2,4,6, ?
  • Q4: Draw a shape pattern using circle and square.
  • Q5: Create a number pattern starting at 5 with +2 steps.

📖 Chapter 11: Tables and Shares

Topics:

  • Multiplication tables (2 to 10 basics)
  • Sharing equally (division basics)
  • Using tables for quick counting
  • Word problems using tables and shares

Examples:

  • 5 table: 5,10,15,20,...
  • 12 sweets ÷ 3 children = 4 sweets each

Questions:

  • Q1: Write 3× table up to 30.
  • Q2: Share 20 apples among 5 kids equally.
  • Q3: Use 4-table to count up to 40.
  • Q4: If 6 boxes have 6 toys each, total toys?
  • Q5: Divide 18 candies among 3 friends.

📖 Chapter 12: How Heavy? How Light?

Topics:

  • Comparing weights using balance
  • Units of weight (kg, g)
  • Estimating heavier and lighter
  • Simple addition/subtraction with weights

Examples:

  • 1 kg = 1000 g. 500 g + 500 g = 1 kg
  • Which is heavier: 2 kg bag or 1500 g bag? (Answer: 2 kg)

Questions:

  • Q1: Convert 2500 g into kg and g.
  • Q2: If mango weighs 300 g, 5 mangoes weigh?
  • Q3: Which is lighter: 750 g or 1 kg?
  • Q4: Add 600 g and 900 g.
  • Q5: Estimate weight of a school bag.

📖 Chapter 13: Fields and Fences

Topics:

  • Measuring area roughly (using squares)
  • Using fence posts to count lengths
  • Relating length and area in simple terms
  • Practical problems (planting, fencing)

Examples:

  • Field of 5 rows each with 6 plots → total plots = 30
  • If fence posts every 1 m for 10 m → 11 posts

Questions:

  • Q1: Count squares in a 4×3 grid → total?
  • Q2: If garden is 6 m long and 4 m wide, how many 1m×1m plots?
  • Q3: How many fence posts needed for 8 m with 1 m gaps?
  • Q4: Draw a small field and divide into equal plots.
  • Q5: Use multiplication to count plots quickly.

📖 Chapter 14: Smart Charts

Topics:

  • Collecting simple data (colors, pets, scores)
  • Making tally marks
  • Drawing bar-like pictorial charts
  • Reading information from a chart

Examples:

  • Tally of favourite fruits: Mango ||| (3), Apple || (2)
  • Bar chart: draw bars for number of students who like different colours

Questions:

  • Q1: Make tally of 10 classmates’ favourite sport.
  • Q2: Draw a pictorial chart for pets at home.
  • Q3: Read and answer: If red=4, blue=2, which colour most liked?
  • Q4: Convert tally marks into numbers.
  • Q5: Make a simple bar chart for number of books read by students.
